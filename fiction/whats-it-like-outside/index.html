Fiction by Rachel Richardson

Rachel Richardson was born in Tulsa, Oklahoma, and now lives in Spartanburg, South Carolina with a large dog, a small dog, and a mid-sized man. She works for Hub City Writers Project and can be found online at www.rachel-richardson.com. She also tweets @pintojamesbean. "What's It Like Outside" is one of 50 short pieces that comprise her book STATE, currently seeking a home. Other STATEÂ pieces can be found at Sundog Lit, Knee-Jerk, Monkeybicycle, and elsewhere.
This piece was inspired by a segment from This American Life where a small town in Kansas was partially destroyed on prom night by an unexpected tornado. When working on my STATE book, I knew I had to pay tribute to tornado alley and I remembered the story. I've never experienced a tornado firsthand, though I did go to both my junior and senior proms (and Winter Formals, Senior Dinner Dance, and far too many others) in Oklahoma. This piece is a mix of others' borrowed history and my own rewritten memories - the rest is pure invention. I am honored and thrilled to have won the Sweet Corn Fiction Contest; the corn, needless to say, was delicious.
